Spine Position

  • Natural Curve: Maintain the natural curves of your spine. Your lower back should have a slight inward curve, while your upper back has a slight outward curve.
  • Upright Posture: Sit or stand tall, as if a string were pulling the crown of your head toward the ceiling. This helps lengthen your spine and prevent slouching.
  • Core Engagement: Gently engage your core muscles to support your spine. This doesn't mean tensing rigidly, but maintaining a natural support for your back.
  • Pelvis Position: Keep your pelvis in a neutral position, not tilted too far forward or backward. This helps maintain proper alignment through your entire spine.
  • Weight Distribution: Whether sitting or standing, distribute your weight evenly to avoid placing undue stress on one side of your spine.
Why Spine Alignment Matters

Proper spine alignment reduces stress on your vertebrae, discs, and surrounding muscles. It allows for optimal breathing, increases your energy levels, and helps prevent common issues like back pain, headaches, and fatigue.
